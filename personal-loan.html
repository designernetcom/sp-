Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) ‚Äì Personal Loan

Q. What is a personal loan?

Ans. A personal loan is an unsecured loan offered by a bank or NBFC to meet your personal financial needs. It is approved based on factors like income, credit history, employment status, and repayment capacity. Unlike home or car loans, no collateral is required. However, this also means interest rates are higher. Missing EMIs or defaulting can hurt your credit score and affect future loan or credit card approvals.

Q. What can a personal loan be used for?

Ans. You can use it for any personal expense, such as:

  • ‚Ä£ Home renovation
  • ‚Ä£ Wedding costs
  • ‚Ä£ Education fees
  • ‚Ä£ Travel or vacations
  • ‚Ä£ Buying gadgets or appliances
  • ‚Ä£ Medical emergencies
  • ‚Ä£ Business investment or car repairs

Banks do not monitor how you use the loan amount.

Q. What is the eligibility criteria?

Ans. Eligibility varies by bank but generally depends on:

  • ‚Ä£ Age: 21 to 65 years
  • ‚Ä£ Income: Regular and stable income (salaried, self-employed, or professional)
  • ‚Ä£ Credit history & employer profile
  • ‚Ä£ Repayment capacity

Q. How quickly is the loan disbursed?

Ans. Most personal loans are disbursed within 7 working days after approval.

The money is either:

  • ‚Ä£ Credited directly to your bank account, or
  • ‚Ä£ Given as an account payee cheque/draft.

Q. How much can I borrow?

The amount depends on your income and existing EMIs:

  • ‚Ä£ Typically, EMIs cannot exceed 40‚Äì50% of your monthly income.
  • ‚Ä£ For self-employed applicants, the amount is based on recent profit & loss statements and other liabilities.

Q. Is there a minimum loan amount?

Q. How do banks decide the maximum loan amount?

Ans. Banks consider:

  • ‚Ä£ Credit score (closer to 900 is best)
  • ‚Ä£ Income & existing liabilities
  • ‚Ä£ Repayment history

A high credit score and lower outstanding debts increase your eligibility and loan amount.

Q. Should I always choose the lowest EMI?

Ans. Not always.

A lower EMI often means a longer tenure, which increases total interest paid.

Use an EMI calculator to check your interest outgo before deciding.

Q. Fixed vs. Floating interest rate ‚Äì what‚Äôs the difference?

Ans. The difference between fixed and floating interest rates lies in how your EMIs are calculated. With a fixed interest rate, your EMI amount remains the same throughout the entire loan tenure, making it predictable and easy to plan your finances. In contrast, a floating interest rate follows the reducing balance method, meaning your EMIs gradually decrease over time as interest is charged only on the outstanding principal amount.

Q. Reducing vs. Flat Interest Rate ‚Äì what‚Äôs the difference?

Ans. The difference between reducing and flat interest rates is in how the interest is calculated. Under the reducing balance method, interest is charged only on the outstanding principal amount, which decreases as you repay the loan, helping you save money over time. In contrast, a flat interest rate charges interest on the entire loan amount for the full tenure, making it more expensive as the interest payable does not reduce even after partial repayments.

Q. How do I repay a loan?

Ans. Loans are typically repaid through EMIs. You can pay via post-dated cheques (PDC) or provide a mandate for automatic payment through the Electronic Clearing Services (ECS) system.

Q. What is the loan approval process?

Ans. Approval is at the discretion of the bank‚Äôs sanctioning officer and depends on the lender‚Äôs eligibility criteria. The process usually takes 48 hours to 2 weeks. Once verification is complete and the loan is sanctioned, the amount is generally disbursed within 7 working days.

Q. What happens if I default on EMIs?

Ans. If you miss EMIs, the lender will first attempt to recover dues through reminders or recovery agents. Continued non-payment will result in your account being marked as a default, which will negatively impact your credit score and make it difficult to get future loans or credit cards.

Q. Are there tax benefits on personal loans?

Ans. Personal loans usually do not offer tax benefits. However, if the loan is taken for home renovation or down payment, you may claim a tax deduction under Section 24 of the Income Tax Act ‚Äì but only on the interest component, not the principal. Proper receipts are required.

Q. What is a balance transfer offer?

Ans. A balance transfer lets you shift your outstanding loan amount from your current lender to another lender offering a lower interest rate. The new lender pays off your existing loan, and you repay them under the new terms. Processing fees and transfer charges may apply.

Q. Why do initial EMIs contribute little to principal repayment?

Ans. In the early stages of repayment, a major portion of your EMI goes toward interest, a process known as front-loading. As time passes and the outstanding principal decreases, a larger share of the EMI goes toward repaying the principal.

Q. What is the difference between a personal loan and a loan against a credit card?

Ans. A credit card loan is a pre-approved offer available only to specific cardholders and does not require additional documentation. A personal loan, on the other hand, can be applied for with any lender and involves document submission and eligibility checks.

Q. What is a credit report and score?

Ans. A credit report is a record of your credit history maintained by agencies like CIBIL TransUnion, Experian, and Equifax. Your credit score (usually on a scale up to 900) reflects your repayment track record. Lenders use it to assess your eligibility for loans.

Q. How does a higher credit score help?

Ans. A higher credit score (750 or above) improves your chances of loan approval and allows you to negotiate benefits such as lower interest rates, higher loan amounts, and processing fee waivers.

Q. Are the required documents the same for all lenders?

Ans. The basic documents (application form, identity proof, address proof, age proof, and income proof) remain the same across lenders. However, self-employed applicants may also need to submit documents like partnership deed, business registration certificate, and business PAN card as proof of business existence.

Q. What is the age limit to get a personal loan?
